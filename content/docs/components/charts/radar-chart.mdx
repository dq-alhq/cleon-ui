## Basic

A radar chart, also known as a spider chart or web chart, is a graphical method of displaying multivariate data in the form of a two-dimensional chart with multiple axes originating from a common center point. Each axis represents a different variable or category, and data points are plotted along each axis to show values relative to each category.

Radar charts are useful for comparing the overall "shape" or pattern of data across multiple variables. They are particularly effective for visualizing strengths and weaknesses across different dimensions or attributes, making them popular in fields such as performance evaluation, market analysis, and sports analytics. Radar charts allow for a quick visual assessment of where certain variables excel or lag behind others, providing a comprehensive overview of complex datasets in a compact and intuitive manner.
